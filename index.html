    Do Something You Love You learn the most by doing something that you love.This is because when you do it, you enjoy yourself so much that you don't realize how much time has passed. Love and creativity are moxed. A hobby like playing an instrument, running, or collecting memorabilia (memorabilia : things you'd collect), can help you chill and fight stress while giving your creativity a boost.
    Just Do Nothing (Trust me it works) Alan Cohen once wrote, "There is virtue in work and there is virtue in rest. Use both and overlook neither." Sometime's when you do nothing, that's when your best ideas start flowing through your mind. It could be at any point in time. While you're taking a shower, driving, hiking, shopping, watching TV or even talking to your best friend. If your creativity seems to be unavailable at the time, then unplug and let your brain do its magic.
    Pick A Terrible Idea And Do It Okay. I know that this sounds stupid, but it works. Make a list of the most useless things and ideas you can think of. There's a chance that while looking at those ideas, a brilliant one will spark.

    Write By Hand By skipping the whole Word Doc process and picking up a pen instead. Sometimes the whole experience of writing by hand seeing the ink on our fingers, the smell of a fresh page in a notebook is all it takes to get creative juices flowing. Hey who knows, maybe while writing, you'll have even better ideas.
    Look at something blue or green These colors tend to increase performance on tasks. Researchers say that’s because whene we see blue we think ocean, sky, and openness in general, while green gives out growing vibes. Check out that globe the next time a problem pops up. Do you feel blue or green?
    Sit OUTSIDE a box This might sound a little strange and wierder than doing nothing, but, in a study, people who sat outside a box (literally) were better at thinking creatively than people who sat in it. So don't throw away those extra boxes you have from amazon. Sit beside it and get those creaive juices flowing.
    Spend Time Alone Hanging with friends can boost creativity, but sometimes a little peace, quiet and me time is necessary. Put on some headphones, get in touch with your inner seld and thoughts, go for a solo walk, or focus on the task at hand.
    Play Video Games Here’s one excuse to sit in front of the computer for hours on end. Video games that motivate players and encourage a good mood (Wii Tennis, not Mortal Combat. Just clearing things up.) can also encourage creativity by raise our problem-solving abilities.
